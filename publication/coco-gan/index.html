COCO-GAN: Conditional Coordinate Generative Adversarial Network

Chieh Hubert Lin , Chia-Che Chang , Yu-Sheng Chen , Da-Cheng Juan , Wei Wei , Hwann-Tzong Chen
Sep 28, 2018

Abstract

Recent advancements on Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) have inspired a wide range of works that generate synthetic images. However, current processes have to generate an entire image at once, and therefore resolutions are limited by memory or computational constraints. In this work, we propose COnditional COordinate GAN (COCO-GAN), which generates a specific patch of an image conditioned on a spatial position rather than the entire image at a time. The generated patches are later combined together to form a globally coherent full-image. We show that the images generated by COCO-GAN can achieve competitive quality to state-of-the-art models, and the generated patches are locally smooth between consecutive neighbors without any post-processing. COCO-GAN unveils the potential of generating high-quality images under conditional coordinated generation and creates a new research direction on spatial separability. Owing to the spatially separable property, COCO-GAN is highly parallelable and enables the next step toward large field-of-view data generation with a built-in divide-and-conquer strategy.
